| ategory                                  | **Past Perfect Simple**                                                                             | **Examples (Past Perfect Simple)**                                     | **Past Perfect Continuous**                                                                                 | **Examples (Past Perfect Continuous)**                                                                    |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Main meaning**                         | Completed action _before another past action or time_.                                              | I had finished my homework before dinner. She had left when I arrived. | Action that _was in progress before another past action_, showing duration.                                 | I had been studying for two hours before the test. They had been waiting for an hour before the bus came. |
|                                          | Emphasizes **result** of the earlier action.                                                        | He was tired because he had worked all day.                            | Emphasizes **duration** or process of the earlier action.                                                   | He was tired because he had been working all day.                                                         |
| **Sequence of actions**                  | The first action (completed earlier in the past).                                                   | When we got to the station, the train had already left.                | Focuses on the ongoing action that led up to another action.                                                | When she arrived, they had been talking for hours.                                                        |
| **Interruptions (short vs long action)** | The shorter or completed action before another.                                                     | I had just closed the window when it started to rain.                  | The longer continuous action interrupted by another.                                                        | It had been raining when the power went out.                                                              |
| **Duration / Repetition**                | Focus on how many times something happened before a point in the past.                              | He had tried three times before he succeeded.                          | Focus on how long something had been happening before a past point.                                         | She had been working there for ten years before she retired.                                              |
| **Temporary situations / Background**    | Completed or finished situations before another event.                                              | I had lived in London before I moved to New York.                      | Ongoing or temporary situations leading up to another event.                                                | I had been living in London before I moved to New York.                                                   |
| **Cause and effect**                     | Shows _reason_ or _result_ of something.                                                            | She was happy because she had passed the exam.                         | Explains _why_ something happened, focusing on ongoing cause.                                               | She was happy because she had been studying so hard.                                                      |
| **Signal words**                         | already, before, after, just, never, once, by the time, until then.                                 | By the time we arrived, the movie had started.                         | for, since, how long, before, until, by the time.                                                           | They had been playing for two hours before it started to rain.                                            |
| **Formation rule**                       | +: Subject + **had + V3 (past participle)** -: Subject + **had not + V3** ?: **Had + subject + V3** | She had finished her work. Had they seen the movie?                    | +: Subject + **had been + V-ing** -: Subject + **had not been + V-ing** ?: **Had + subject + been + V-ing** | He had been reading. Had you been waiting long?                                                           |
