
| **Category**                         | **Present Perfect Simple**                                                                                            | **Examples (Present Perfect Simple)**                | **Past Simple**                                                                                 | **Examples (Past Simple)**                                     |
| ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Main meaning**                     | Action that happened **at an unspecified time before now**, result visible **in the present**.                        | I have lost my keys. (I don’t have them now.)        | Action that happened **at a specific time in the past**, finished and **not connected to now**. | I lost my keys yesterday. (I found them later / end of story.) |
| **Time reference**                   | **No exact time** mentioned (ever, never, already, yet, just, recently, so far).                                      | She has just arrived.                                | **Exact time** in the past (yesterday, last week, in 2010, when I was a child).                 | She arrived yesterday.                                         |
| **Connection to now**                | The result or experience **matters now**.                                                                             | I’ve visited Rome twice.                             | The action is **completely in the past**, no link to now.                                       | I visited Rome in 2018.                                        |
| **Use with unfinished time periods** | Used with **today, this week, this year** when the period isn’t over.                                                 | I’ve seen him three times this week.                 | Used with **finished time periods** (last week, last month, etc.).                              | I saw him three times last week.                               |
| **Experience**                       | Describes **life experience** (no time given).                                                                        | Have you ever been to Japan?                         | Describes **past events** with a known time.                                                    | Did you go to Japan in 2019?                                   |
| **Recent actions / news**            | Used to announce or emphasize **recent events**.                                                                      | They’ve just moved to a new flat.                    | Used for **past events with clear time context**.                                               | They moved to a new flat last month.                           |
| **Formation rule**                   | +: Subject + **have/has + V3 (past participle)** -: Subject + **haven’t/hasn’t + V3** ?: **Have/Has + subject + V3?** | She has finished her work. Have you seen that movie? | +: Subject + **V2/ed** -: Subject + **did not + V1** ?: **Did + subject + V1?**                 | She finished her work. Did you see that movie?                 |
| **Signal words**                     | ever, never, already, yet, just, since, for, recently, so far, today, this week.                                      | Have you finished yet?                               | yesterday, last week, ago, in 2010, when, then, after.                                          | I met him two days ago.                                        |
